# 📊 Insights & Recommendations

## 🔍 Key Insights
- ~480 high-risk customers (~5%) show the highest churn likelihood  
- Customers with 1 product (~50%) contribute most to churn  
- Inactive customers (~35–40%) have significantly higher churn rates  
- Germany shows higher churn (~32%) compared to other regions
-  Customers in the 46-50 age group exhibit the highest churn rate (~50%)  

---

## 💡 Recommendations
- Target high-risk customers with retention campaigns  
- Cross-sell to single-product customers to improve engagement  
- Focus on reactivating inactive customers  
- Prioritize medium-risk segment to reduce overall churn  

---

## 📈 Summary
Customer churn is primarily driven by low engagement. Targeting high-risk and medium-risk segments with focused strategies can significantly improve retention and reduce revenue loss.
